# Research Direction Protocol

Use this reference only for Mission intake, Portfolio construction, Study
diagnosis, architecture review, or result-driven follow-up.

## 1. Intake Review

Read the exact Journal/index head and reconstruct the research forest from:

1. Study questions and changed/controlled variables.
2. Final validator evidence and typed Study outcomes.
3. Study KPI rows as navigation only.
4. Negative memory and reopen conditions.
   Read additive historical adjudications before treating a whole-Executable
   memory as a claim-level ban.
5. Portfolio Decisions and omitted alternatives.
6. Executable component protocols and common architecture.
7. Mission terminals and exhaustion bases.

Produce a coverage map with these columns:

`Study | causal question | primary layer | architecture family | changed | controlled | outcome | evidence state | reopen condition`

Do not guess missing legacy tags. Mark them `legacy_unclassified`, then use the
underlying question and component manifests for qualitative findings without
rewriting old authority.

## 2. Layer And Architecture Separation

`ResearchLayer` answers: which system layer is the primary causal contrast?

- `data_source`: new point-in-time information or executable external input.
- `feature`: representation of information already available.
- `label`: target event, horizon, path, barrier, or survival definition.
- `model`: fitted functional form or capacity.
- `objective`: fitted loss or utility target.
- `calibration`: score-to-probability or abstention calibration.
- `selector`: entry threshold, rank, or gating policy.
- `trade`: direction, order, or entry policy.
- `lifecycle`: exit and position-state evolution.
- `risk`: exposure and risk control under fixed discovery sizing.
- `regime`: conditional state segmentation or routing context.
- `execution`: fill, cost, liquidity, and executable timing semantics.
- `synthesis`: multi-component composition.
- `portfolio`: sleeve routing, complementarity, and exposure netting.

`system_architecture_family` answers: which common prediction-to-position
chassis would make otherwise different axes share the same hidden assumption?
Use a stable ASCII identifier such as:

`architecture-family:directional-next-open-fixed-horizon-fixed-hold`

Do not rename an architecture merely to avoid a review trigger. Change it only
when label, decision, entry, lifecycle, execution, or portfolio semantics make
the chassis materially different.
Define the family from stable semantic roles and boundary bindings. Keep actual
implementation bytes in Component and Executable identity; a harmless refactor
must not manufacture an unrelated architecture family.

## 3. Axis Admission

Admit an axis only when all are explicit:

- one causal question;
- one primary research layer, except typed synthesis or Portfolio work;
- changed and controlled domains;
- stable architecture family;
- why the axis has information value now;
- simplest decisive baseline;
- acceptance, falsification, stop, and reopen conditions;
- relation to prior negative memory and semantic warnings.

Layer diversity is not calendar rotation. Prefer the bottleneck with the
highest expected information value, while keeping the initial Portfolio and
Mission exhaustion claim structurally credible.

## 4. Evidence-State Diagnosis

Choose the narrowest state supported by the final bound evidence:

| Evidence state | Diagnostic question |
| --- | --- |
| `absent_information` | Did the mechanism fail to separate from causal controls? |
| `target_mismatch` | Does information exist but disagree with target shape, horizon, or path? |
| `model_capacity` | Do preregistered residual or interaction tests support missing capacity? |
| `calibration_selection` | Is ranking useful while calibration or abstention loses utility? |
| `entry_policy` | Is the signal useful but decision timing or direction selection wrong? |
| `lifecycle_risk` | Is entry useful while exit, path, or risk destroys the result? |
| `execution_cost` | Is gross utility present but native cost or execution removes it? |
| `stability_concentration` | Is aggregate utility concentrated by time, side, regime, or sleeve? |
| `supported_requires_confirmation` | Did the exact Study pass but still require independent depth? |
| `not_identifiable` | Could the design not distinguish the causal alternatives? |
| `engineering_gap` | Did non-scientific failure prevent a scientific judgment? |

Before selecting the row, separate validity, component support, multiplicity,
and diagnostic risk. Missing or invalid validity evidence is not evaluable.
Discovery concentration profile B04 is diagnostic unless preregistered as a
decisive gate. A failed familywise gate can leave supported components as a
partial positive, but never creates candidate authority.

Never diagnose `model_capacity` merely because a larger model is available.
Never diagnose `target_mismatch` merely because another label might score
better. The evidence must identify that bottleneck or the diagnosis confidence
must remain low with a decisive counterfactual.

The writer, not the operator, derives allowed follow-up actions and layers.

## 5. Result-Driven Branching

- No information: prune or rotate to genuinely different information.
- Target mismatch: design a label or objective contrast with the prior signal
  and chassis controlled.
- Capacity evidence: compare the smallest model-capacity increase against the
  existing model.
- Calibration or selector evidence: hold signal and outcome fixed.
- Entry evidence: hold signal and lifecycle fixed where possible.
- Lifecycle or risk evidence: hold entry identity fixed.
- Gross-to-net failure: hold signal fixed and test execution or density.
- Stability or concentration failure: test regime, risk, synthesis, or
  complementary sleeves.
- Supported evidence: run independent confirmation; do not immediately crown a
  candidate.
- Not identifiable: redesign the contrast before tuning.
- Engineering gap: Repair or preserve the question; grant no scientific credit.

An unrelated high-information-value axis remains valid as a structural forest
exit. It must differ in primary layer or architecture family and use a
diversifying Decision action. When several live axes share one boundary,
compare and execute them as one preregistered concurrent forest rather than
serializing them by recency.

## 6. Architecture Review

Review only the exact diagnosis set named by the writer trigger. Determine
whether the shared chassis, rather than each mechanism independently, is the
current bottleneck. Choose exactly one typed conclusion:

- `bounded_same_architecture`: use only when the quant team identifies one
  bounded and testable continuation that either exclusion conclusion would
  wrongly suppress. Bind the exact reviewed family, trigger, covered diagnosis
  set, and stop or reopen condition. Select one closed mode:
  - `existing_axis`: bind one exact currently selectable axis ID and immutable
    axis identity under the reviewed family;
  - `new_mechanism`: bind one expert-selected typed `ResearchLayer`; the next
    Decision must add a genuinely distinct mechanism in the exact reviewed
    family and layer.
- `change_research_layer`: require the next selected or newly admitted axis to
  leave the reviewed layers.
- `rotate_architecture`: require the next selected or newly admitted axis to
  leave the reviewed architecture family.

Use ArchitectureReview v2 only for `bounded_same_architecture`; preserve legacy
v1 identity for the two exclusion conclusions. The bounded review and the next
QuantTeam Decision review must cite the current Portfolio snapshot, exact
review, trigger, and every covered diagnosis. Free-form exceptions, an
unselectable existing axis, a caller-invented family, or a new mechanism in a
different layer cannot satisfy the direction. Bind every review identity to
the exact `trigger_record_id` so a later review cannot collide with or
impersonate the conclusion.

Do not declare scientific exhaustion from the review itself. Mission exhaustion
still requires the current evidence-bound disposition for every axis and the
required diversity across mechanisms, research layers, and architectures.
Partial, invalid, or unresolved evidence needs a replay, preservation,
low-information disposition, or explicit reopen rule; do not manufacture a
negative merely to satisfy a terminal count.
