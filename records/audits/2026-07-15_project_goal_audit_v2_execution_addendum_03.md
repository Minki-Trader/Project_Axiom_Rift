## Findings And Repairs

### V2-E13: shared-cache consumers reserved full producer cost

The first four Jobs completed, but declaration of the fifth Job was rejected
because each member reserved 3,600 compute seconds and 5,400 wall seconds.  The
frozen Batch ceiling therefore made every family larger than four members
impossible, even though only one producer performs the expensive computation
and consumers validate bounded cached outputs.  This was an operational
bottleneck, not scientific evidence, and the completed Jobs were preserved.

The reusable workflow now assigns role-specific reservations under
`fixed_hold_replay.shared_family_cache.v2`: 3,600 compute and 5,400 wall for the
single producer, and 900 compute and 1,440 wall for each cache consumer.  The
twelve-member family reserves 13,500 compute and 21,240 wall, below the existing
14,400 and 21,600 ceilings.  No ceiling, stop rule, family, trial limit,
Executable, evidence, verdict, or candidate authority was loosened.

### V2-E14: frozen over-reservation had no safe correction path

Increasing the Batch ceiling or reopening completed Jobs would have hidden the
design error and weakened the frozen protocol.  A typed, reduction-only Batch
budget Repair now operates only at a stable between-Job boundary.  It requires
the registered workflow policy, one exact cache producer, the same callable and
implementation closure, completed or judged prior Jobs, and an exact proof
artifact.  It cannot increase reservations, erase them to zero, alter the
scientific family, or create a trial.  Arbitrary lower numbers are rejected.
