## Purpose And Boundary

This is the required second exhaustive audit after the first audit correction
and the STU-0105 execution. It tests the complete operating chain rather than
only recent research: persistent direction, contracts, skills, canonical state,
Journal and index projections, historical Studies and Executables, source
authority, evidence construction, validator semantics, Portfolio and terminal
readers, implementation identity, delivery checkpoints, test selection, and
validation economics.

STU-0105 was a real executed audit reanalysis, but its purpose was historical
reconstruction. This second pass does not promote it into prospective causal,
temporal, regime, stress, cost, economic, or terminal evidence. The exact P1
scientific replay remains pending. This report changes no canonical state,
trial, verdict, holdout, candidate, Release, or Project Goal terminal by itself.

No quarantine or holdout values were read. Protected ignored data and the
unrelated untracked TLT work were not used as audit evidence or modified.

## Method

The audit followed the repository from its persistent authority through the
current stable boundary and checked the following linked surfaces:

1. Every authority and routing layer that can change scientific behavior.
2. Canonical state and the immutable Journal path through revision 4935.
3. Historical adjudications, evidence-mode claims, replay dispositions, source
   invalidations, Portfolio snapshots, axis readers, and terminal credit.
4. Reusable research engines and the historical hardcoded runner population.
5. Plan, measurement, proof, trace, result, and economic attribution surfaces.
6. Component, Executable, Job, and implementation-content identity closure.
7. Study-close KPI, Git authentication, checkpoint, and delivery repair paths.
8. Routine, full-maintenance, exact-staging, and tracked-test validation paths.

The method used immutable identities and provenance. It did not infer a correct
result from a display name, prior verdict, cached summary, or current broker
reconstruction. Engineering defects were separated from scientific evidence.

## Findings And Bound Repairs

### V2-01 P0: evidence-mode labels were treated as capabilities

STU-0105 declared causal, cost, stress, and temporal modes even though its
purpose and available artifacts established audit reconstruction. Cached point
values and declared mode names could therefore flow into historical mode counts
without the underlying registered protocol being recomputed.

Bound repair:

- a mode label, caller declaration, prior verdict, or cache is not capability;
- audit reanalysis has only `audit_integrity` effective scope;
- audit integrity has zero scientific, economic, exhaustion, candidate, and
  terminal credit;
- an additive completion-bound scope overlay corrects readers without rewriting
  the STU-0105 completion;
- the pending P1 obligation can be satisfied only by its exact prospective
  protocol and original subject-bound criteria.

### V2-02 P0: summary artifacts could substitute for atomic proof

Historical replay surfaces retained summary metrics and Monte Carlo values but
did not require a durable atomic support artifact, statistical proof, execution
trace, and result to be opened and recomputed as one exact bundle. Decision-day
and exit-day attribution, native and stressed costs, and family resampling could
therefore be implied rather than demonstrated.

Bound repair:

- every scientific proof binds validation plan, atomic support, statistical
  proof, execution trace, and result manifest;
- the validator opens every artifact and recomputes the registered protocol;
- support binds protocol, data, split, and Executable identities;
- statistical proof binds exact concurrent family, method, seed, resample
  count, alpha, raw and adjusted statistics, and Monte Carlo uncertainty;
- the trace binds decision, entry and exit time, fold, regime, intent, gross
  PnL, native and stress cost, net PnL, and explicit result attribution.

### V2-03 P0: comparison state and scientific state were conflated

A raw threshold comparison could be passed or failed while its scientific role
was diagnostic, invalid, unresolved, supported, or contradicted. Readers that
used the comparison field could turn a diagnostic check into scientific credit
or a whole-claim failure.

Bound repair:

- comparison state is limited to passed, failed, or not_evaluable;
- scientific state remains supported, contradicted, unresolved, invalid, or
  diagnostic;
- scheduler, negative-memory, exhaustion, and terminal readers use only the
  scientific state and exact decisive role.

### V2-04 P0: invalid source axes remained visible as ordinary live axes

The immutable Portfolio snapshot correctly preserved historical axes, but
downstream readers could treat that snapshot as current eligibility after an
additive source invalidation. This allowed an audit-invalidated source axis to
appear selectable or terminal-creditable before its typed resolution.

Bound repair:

- Portfolio snapshots remain immutable observations;
- a separate effective-axis projection applies source invalidations, replay
  obligations, and evidence-scope overlays;
- an invalidated source axis is excluded from scheduling and terminal credit
  until its exact typed resolution policy is satisfied.

### V2-05 P0/P1: replay had no complete typed lifecycle or exact member binding

Thirteen historical adjudications require bounded replay treatment: six P0
audit-integrity corrections and seven P1 prospective scientific obligations.
The earlier path could bind a multi-Executable replay through the first trial,
which is not valid member-level evidence.

Bound repair:

- each obligation has P0 or P1 priority and pending, in_progress, satisfied, or
  deferred state;
- each binds the exact original Study, adjudication, Executable, criteria, and
  satisfaction or defer condition;
- each new Executable can match at most one exact original Executable;
- position, display order, first-trial, Study-wide, and family-level fallback
  cannot satisfy an unmatched member;
- P0 blocks the affected credit; P1 receives the highest-information-value
  bounded opportunity without freezing unrelated valid forest work.

The next prospective target is the exact STU-0061 P1 obligation. Its concurrent
four-member analog family and controls must be evaluated together under the
registered protocol and all twenty original criteria must be recomputed. This
audit does not claim that replay has run or passed.

### V2-06 P1: a composite label could overstate economic evidence

A combined diagnostic or significance surface could be called a composite even
when it had not executed the actual member trades and portfolio interactions.
That is useful scientific context but not composite economics.

Bound repair:

- economic composite evidence requires actual member trade rows;
- entry and exit timing, exposure netting, native and stressed costs, and
  portfolio drawdown attribution are mandatory;
- a component-summary bundle or combined significance label is not economic
  evidence.

### V2-07 P1: implementation identity did not close the full execution chain

Fixed implementation labels and copied engine identities could attest changed
code without proving the current participating bytes. The repository also
contains 52 hardcoded historical research modules whose embedded Mission or
Study identities make them unsuitable as prospective engines.

Bound repair:

- Component identity binds current bytes and semantic dependencies;
- Executable identity contains every participating Component identity;
- Job implementation evidence covers every participating implementation;
- fixed or caller-provided identities without current-byte closure are rejected;
- the 52 hardcoded modules are frozen historical reconstruction surfaces;
- prospective research uses context-bound reusable engines and declarative
  plans; retirement still requires exact parity evidence.

### V2-08 P1: historical delivery proof was not inside checkpoint authority

Checkpoint v1 bounded the prospective suffix but did not bind the complete
twenty-one-row historical KPI backfill proof. Routine validation and complete
maintenance were also insufficiently separated, and close/no-close monotonicity
needed an explicit invariant.

Bound repair:

- checkpoint v2 binds the exact twenty-one-source backfill set;
- each source binds original close event, authenticated commit, tree, required
  path blobs, trailer or typed attestation, local-main ancestry, and deterministic
  KPI bytes;
- v1 to v2 upgrade is an explicit full-maintenance action;
- routine validation is a fast bounded suffix check;
- complete reconstruction and exact staging are explicit modes;
- close and no-close boundaries are monotone and no-close cannot advance the
  authenticated close count.

### V2-09 P1: real-worktree Git checks had silent absence paths

Some delivery guards could return when `.git` or HEAD was absent. That behavior
is acceptable only for an explicitly isolated fixture, not for a real scientific
worktree.

Bound repair:

- missing repository, HEAD, local main, hook, checkpoint, or authenticated path
  fails closed on a real worktree;
- only a typed isolated engineering fixture can omit those surfaces;
- a fixture remains trial-zero, candidate-ineligible, and scientifically
  ineligible.

### V2-10 P1: repository-wide tests could be contaminated by untracked work

A raw filesystem test discovery could include unrelated untracked tests, while
the intended committed acceptance surface is the Git-tracked repository. This
made test count and acceptance authority dependent on local user work.

Bound repair:

- repository-wide engineering runs use a Git-index-bound test manifest;
- each included path and byte identity is recorded;
- excluded untracked tests are reported explicitly;
- an intended new test is staged before it can enter the manifest;
- unrelated untracked user work is never acceptance authority.

### V2-11 P1: validation could delay experiments without protecting a claim

Repeated full recovery, full-history delivery audit, or full-suite discovery at
every boundary adds latency that does not scale with the current claim. It can
turn a safety harness into a research bottleneck.

Bound repair:

- focused checks and fast current-surface guards are routine;
- full history, tracked full suite, and exact staging run at coherent maintenance
  or delivery milestones;
- unchanged validation is reused by identity;
- a permitted bounded scientific Job does not wait for unrelated slow checks;
- tests remain engineering evidence, not scientific evidence.

### V2-12 P1: one-dimensional adjudication could replace professional judgment

The project already names multidisciplinary roles, but a rigid scalar or serial
policy can still turn them into one effective decision rule. This is inconsistent
with a professional quant team and forest-first search.

Bound repair:

- material decisions use causal, statistical, data, execution, economic, risk,
  and architecture lenses;
- disagreement is resolved at claim boundaries with evidence and opportunity
  cost;
- the harness enforces identity, permits, budgets, and claim scope, but does not
  choose the scientific allocation;
- no universal scalar, rigid calendar rotation, or serial recent-winner policy
  replaces bounded autonomous judgment.

### V2-13 P2: software concentration obscured independent invariants

Writer, proof, and delivery responsibilities had grown into large surfaces, and
an execution-frame construction path emitted a pandas concatenation warning.
These are engineering quality and throughput risks, not scientific results.

Bound repair:

- replay, effective-axis, scope-overlay, checkpoint backfill, delivery, trace,
  and proof responsibilities are separated into focused modules;
- shared typed trade-frame construction replaces warning-prone ad hoc empty
  concatenation;
- semantic behavior remains identity-bound and any scientific change still
  creates a new Executable.

### V2-14 P2: a reconstructible SQLite projection polluted the worktree

A real stable-head read created `state/index.sqlite3`. Its SQLite database,
write-ahead log, and shared-memory companions are local reconstructible
projections, not durable authority, but the paths were not ignored. A read-only
operational check could therefore create unrelated untracked worktree state.

Bound repair:

- local SQLite projection, WAL, and SHM paths are ignored;
- the projection is rebuilt from the durable Journal when absent or invalid;
- projection presence or bytes never establish scientific or state authority;
- tracked authority remains the control, Journal, KPI, and exact authenticated
  delivery surfaces rather than a disposable SQLite file.
